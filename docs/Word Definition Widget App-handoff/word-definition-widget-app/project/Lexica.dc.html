adjective
/əˈfem(ə)rəl/
ephemeral
DEFINITION
Lasting for a markedly brief time. Used especially of things whose beauty or relevance is brief.
EXAMPLE
“The ephemeral beauty of cherry blossom draws crowds each spring.”
SYNONYMS
fleeting transient passing
ANTONYMS
permanent enduring lasting
ORIGIN
Mid 16th century: from Greek ephēmeros ‘lasting only a day’.
